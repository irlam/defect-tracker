Guide: Uploading Files to Google Drive After a Defect is Closed for 7 Days

Introduction

This guide provides detailed instructions on how to upload files to Google Drive after a defect has been closed for 7 days. Follow the steps below to ensure a smooth and efficient upload process.

Prerequisites

  • A Google account with access to Google Drive.
  • The file(s) you wish to upload.
  • The defect must have been closed for at least 7 days.

Steps to Create credentials.json and Set Up Google API Client

1. Enable the Google Drive API

  1. Go to the Google Cloud Console.
  2. Create a new project or select an existing project.
  3. In the left sidebar, click on APIs & Services > Library.
  4. Search for "Google Drive API" and click on it.
  5. Click the Enable button.

2. Create OAuth 2.0 Credentials

  1. In the left sidebar, click on APIs & Services > Credentials.
  2. Click on the Create Credentials button and select OAuth 2.0 Client IDs.
  3. Configure the consent screen by clicking on Configure consent screen if prompted.
  4. Choose External and click on Create.
  5. Fill out the required fields (App name, User support email, etc.) and save the configuration.
  6. Select Application type as Web application.
  7. Enter a name for your OAuth 2.0 Client ID.
  8. In the Authorized redirect URIs section, add the following URI:
    • http://YOUR_DOMAIN/google_upload/oauth_callback.php
    Replace YOUR_DOMAIN with your actual domain name.
  9. Click on Create.
  10. Download the credentials.json file and save it to your local machine.

3. Upload credentials.json to Your Server

  1. Using an FTP client or your hosting provider's file manager, upload the credentials.json file to the following directory on your server:
    • /var/www/vhosts/hosting215226.ae97b.netcup.net/mcgoff.defecttracker.uk/httpdocs/google_upload/

Steps to Upload Files to Google Drive

1. Verify Defect Closure

Ensure that the defect has been closed for at least 7 days. You can check the closure date in the defect tracking system.

2. Prepare Files for Upload

Gather all the files you need to upload to Google Drive. Ensure they are properly named and organized.

3. Access Google Drive

  1. Open your web browser and navigate to Google Drive.
  2. Log in with your Google account credentials.

4. Create a New Folder (Optional)

If you want to organize your files, you can create a new folder in Google Drive.

  1. Click on the "New" button in the top left corner.
  2. Select "Folder" from the dropdown menu.
  3. Enter a name for the folder and click "Create."

5. Upload Files to Google Drive

  1. Click on the "New" button in the top left corner.
  2. Select "File upload" or "Folder upload" from the dropdown menu, depending on your needs.
  3. Navigate to the location of your files on your computer and select the files or folder you want to upload.
  4. Click "Open" to start the upload process.

6. Verify Upload

Once the upload is complete, verify that all files have been successfully uploaded to Google Drive. You should see the uploaded files in your Drive.

Additional Tips

  • Ensure you have a stable internet connection during the upload process.
  • If you encounter any issues, refer to the Google Drive Help Center for troubleshooting tips.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you can efficiently upload your files to Google Drive after a defect has been closed for 7 days. If you have any questions or need further assistance, please contact support.
